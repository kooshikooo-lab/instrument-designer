### 2.3 Tone Hole Principles

**Cutoff Frequency:**
- Frequency above which sound waves ignore open tone holes
- High cutoff = bright tone; low cutoff = dark tone

**Factors Affecting Tone (Cutoff):**
- Large holes + short spacing + shallow holes + high pads → HIGH cutoff → BRIGHT
- Small holes + wide spacing + deep holes + low pads → LOW cutoff → DARK

**Undercutting (Fraising):**
- Making a hole behave acoustically larger while maintaining smaller visual appearance
- Darkens tone
- Common in professional instruments

## 3. REGISTER HOLE / SPEAKER KEY

### 3.1 Position & Dimensions

| Instrument | Register Hole Position | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bb Clarinet | ~140-160 mm from mouthpiece tip | Radius ~1.5 mm, chimney height ~13 mm |
| Bass Clarinet | Two register vents (automatic switching) | Large vent (lower) + small pip (upper) |

**Double Register Key System (Professional Bass Clarinets):**
- **Lower register vent:** Opens for low-clarion B through Eb (large vent on body)
- **Upper register vent:** Opens for clarion E and above (small neck/body pip)
- Automatic switching between the two

### 3.2 Brand-Specific Register Key Details

| Brand | Register Key Design | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Buffet** | Extra vent hole for throat Bb (Tosca) | Register hole placed further down → better E/B 12th tuning |
| **Buffet** | Register tube ~0.580" long | May benefit from enlarging (3 mm → 3.5 mm for some players) |
| **Selmer** | Standard double register | Clean mechanism on Privilege |
| **Yamaha** | Standard double register | — |

### 3.3 Register Hole Principles

**Ideal Position:** ~1/3 distance from the reed end

**Purpose:**
- Suppresses even harmonics (4th, 8th, etc.)
- Forces instrument to overblow to the 12th (not the octave)
- Placed at a pressure node of the desired register

## 4. LOW C vs LOW Eb COMPARISON

### 4.1 Physical Dimensions

| Dimension | Low Eb | Low C |
|---|---|---|
| Lower joint length | Standard | ~8 inches (200 mm) longer |
| Right-side pad cups | 2-3 large cups | 5-6 large cups |
| Right thumb keys | None | 3 thumb keys (D, C#, C) |
| Total keys | 18-20 keys | 24 keys |
| Lowest note | Written Eb (concert Db) | Written C (concert Bb) |
| Weight | ~3.5 kg (~7.7 lbs) | ~3.9 kg (~8.6 lbs) |
| Case size | Alto sax | Tenor sax |

### 4.2 Key Mechanism Differences

**Low C Bass Clarinet:**
- Right hand thumb keys under thumb rest for D, C#, and C
- 6 spatula keys for right pinky (vs 3-4 on low Eb)
- Double D spatula key (Buffet)
- Rollers on low C-C# (Selmer Privilege, Buffet Tosca)

**Low Eb Bass Clarinet:**
- Low E tone hole on bell (metal bell)
- Low C instruments have this on body

### 4.3 Acoustic Differences

| Aspect | Low C | Low Eb |
|---|---|---|
| Low Eb note quality | Better (body note, not bell) | Bell note (slightly less stable) |
| Mid-clarion response (E, F, F#) | Slightly better | Standard |
| Low note projection | Slightly less (more tube) | Slightly more (shorter tube) |
| Intonation | Slightly better overall | Good |
| Response speed | Slightly slower (longer tube) | Slightly faster |

## 6. BORE TAPER DETAILS

### 6.1 Bass Clarinet Bore Profile

```
NECK: Conical (tapering toward top)
     |
UPPER JOINT: Primarily cylindrical (23.0-24.0 mm)
  - Some enlargement around register hole region
  - Polycylindrical in Selmer/Buffet
     |
LOWER JOINT: Long gradual flare
  - From Ab/Eb tone hole downward
  - Narrows wide twelfths at bottom
     |
METAL BELL: Conical flare (only section with both inside and outside taper)
```

### 6.2 Bore Taper Principles

1. **Mouthpiece bore** - Usually conical; affects overall pitch and balance
2. **Barrel bore** - Affects tuning of upper 2nd register and lower altissimo
   - French style = reverse conical (tapering toward bottom)
3. **Upper joint** - Enlarged around register hole to bring middle twelfths into tune
4. **Lower joint** - Long gradual flare from Ab/Eb hole downward
5. **Metal bell** - Only conical section (both inside and out)

## 8. OPEN-HOLE vs PLATEAU KEYS

### 8.1 Bass Clarinet Key Style

All modern bass clarinets use **covered (plateau) finger holes with pads**

- Open-hole bass clarinets are essentially non-existent in production
- Some rare custom instruments may exist

### 8.2 Covered Hole Advantages

- Consistent sealing regardless of finger position
- Ability to use keywork for all tone holes
- Pad height affects cutoff frequency and response
  - Higher pad opening → brighter tone
  - Lower pad → darker tone

## 13. GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Bore** | Internal diameter of the clarinet tube |
| **Polycylindrical** | Bore with multiple cylindrical sections of slightly different diameters |
| **Cutoff frequency** | Frequency above which open tone holes are acoustically transparent |
| **Undercutting** | Angling the interior of a tone hole to make it acoustically larger |
| **Register hole** | Small vent hole that suppresses even harmonics, forcing overblowing to 12th |
| **Chimney** | The height of a tone hole (distance from bore to pad) |
| **Tenon** | Joint connecting sections of the clarinet |
| **Pad cup** | Key mechanism that covers a tone hole |
| **Spatula key** | Flat key for pinky finger |
| **Green LinE** | Buffet's material made from grenadilla powder and resin |

# Research: Cross-Fingering Design for Bass Clarinet Chromatic Scale
## Goal: Design a fingering chart for ~12 toneholes that achieves <5c chromatic intonation

## Context
We have confirmed that SEQUENTIAL fingerings (open one more hole per note) are PHYSICALLY LIMITED to ~15c RMS for a 12-hole chromatic scale on a bass clarinet (25mm bore, 11mm holes, 1211mm effective length). The limit comes from the fundamental physics: at 73-150Hz, open toneholes have high shunt impedance (Q>>100) and don't change the effective length enough to produce accurate semitones near the reed end.

Real clarinets (Boehm, Oehler systems) solve this with:
1. Cross-fingerings (forked fingerings with open-close-open patterns)
2. Graduated hole sizes (7-9mm upper, 10-12mm lower)
3. Specific fingerings refined over 150+ years

## The Challenge
Design a fingering chart for a 12-tonehole + 1 register hole instrument where:
- Hole positions are OPTIMIZABLE (can be placed anywhere along the bore)
- Register hole is at ~80mm from reed (fixed)
- Each note specifies which holes are open/closed
- Target: chromatic scale D2 (73Hz) through D3 (147Hz), 13 notes

## Key Acoustic Principles
1. The FIRST open hole (closest to reed) sets the approximate effective length
2. A CLOSED hole BETWEEN two open holes creates a cavity that shifts resonance slightly higher (shorter effective length)
3. The REGISTER hole (if open) at ~1/3 of bore acts as a vent favoring the 3rd harmonic
4. Graduated hole sizes: 7-9mm near reed, 10-12mm near bell

## Questions
1. For a 12-hole instrument, what is the optimal arrangement of diatonic vs chromatic holes? (E.g., 6 diatonic + 6 chromatic interspersed, or 7+5, or 12 sequential?)
2. Which specific notes benefit from cross-fingerings vs sequential?
3. What are the classic clarinet cross-fingerings for the chalumeau register and how do they map to our 12-hole model?
4. Can you provide a COMPLETE fingering chart (13 notes × 13 binary entries) with open/closed for each hole?
5. Should some notes use the 2nd register (register key open) instead of cross-fingerings?

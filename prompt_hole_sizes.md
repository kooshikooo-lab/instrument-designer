# Research: Graduated Hole Sizes for Bass Clarinet
## Goal: Determine optimal hole size graduation from small (reed) to large (bell)

## Context
Our current model uses uniform 11mm holes on a 25mm bore (25mm ID).
Real bass clarinets use smaller holes near the reed (8-10mm) and larger near the bell (14-18mm).
The acoustic effectiveness of a tonehole depends on the area ratio S_hole/S_bore.
At low frequencies (70-150Hz), smaller holes become ineffective as vents.

## Observed Behavior
- 11mm holes on 25mm bore: S_hole/S_bore = 0.19 (19%). Works for 2nd register.
- 20mm holes on 25mm bore: S_hole/S_bore = 0.64 (64%). Works for both registers.
- Real clarinet (14mm bore, 12mm hole): S_hole/S_bore = 0.73 (73%). Ideal.

## Questions
1. What is the minimum S_hole/S_bore ratio for effective venting at the fundamental?
2. What is the standard hole size graduation for bass clarinet (by hole number)?
3. Is there a rule of thumb or formula for hole size vs. position?
4. Should we optimize hole diameters AND positions simultaneously?
5. What is the effect of hole chimney length (pad height) on tuning?
6. Does a graduated bore (taper) help with hole effectiveness at low frequencies?
7. What are the mechanical constraints on hole size (key spacing, pad cup size)?

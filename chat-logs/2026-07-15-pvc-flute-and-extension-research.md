## 1. PVC Flute Projects by Type

### 1.1 Transverse Flutes (Side-Blown)

#### PVC Concert Flute (Key of C)
- **Material:** 3/4" Schedule 40 PVC pipe (ID: 20.9mm)
- **Length:** ~30cm (soprano range)
- **Design:** Side-blown embouchure hole, 6 finger holes
- **Source:** University of Rochester PHY103 Lab (astro.pas.rochester.edu)
- **Notes:** PVC produces a "lovely soft bamboo-like sound". Cork at closed end.
- **Key specs:**
  - Blow hole: 3/8" (9.5mm), positioned 3cm from cork end
  - Finger holes: 11/32" (8.7mm) diameter
  - Hole spacing calculated from frequency ratios

#### PVC Irish/ Folk Flute (Key of D)
- **Material:** 3/4" Schedule 40 PVC pipe
- **Design:** Simple-system flute, 6 holes, keyless
- **Source:** pfh (Paul Harrison) Thingiverse designs
- **Note:** Tapered bore possible with heated/rolled PVC

#### PVC Native American Flute
- **Material:** 3/4" PVC pipe + 3D printed fetish/air director
- **Source:** PhreshAyer YouTube, University of Oregon DeArmond Makerspace
- **Key features:**
  - Fipple-style (whistle mouthpiece), not transverse
  - 3D printed air-flow director: 25 x 15 x 6mm rectangle with 2 x 10 x 18mm slot
  - Wine cork plug
  - Tuned in key of G
  - MUCH easier to play than transverse flutes
- **Build time:** ~30 min with drill, half day without
- **Cost:** ~$5 in materials
- **Critical:** Use Schedule 40 white PVC (food-grade), NOT gray drainage pipe

### 1.2 Fipple Flutes (Recorder-style)

#### PVC Recorder (Soprano, Key of C)
- **Material:** 3/4" PVC pipe + 3D printed mouthpiece
- **Source:** CamMan (CameronBilli) on Printables - "3/4\" PVC Flute Recorder Mouthpiece"
- **URL:** https://www.printables.com/model/368998
- **Design:** 3D printed fipple mouthpiece fits onto standard 3/4" PVC pipe
- **Notes:** Combine with pfh Demakein-generated bore profiles

#### PVC Great Bass Recorder
- **Material:** PVC pipe body + 3D printed fipple cap, hinges, and keys
- **Source:** ssngai on Instructables
- **Range:** Great bass (C below middle C), 4 feet long
- **Design:** 3D printing reserved for crucial parts (fipple cap, hinges, two keys)
- **Material:** PVC body + Shapeways "White Strong and Flexible" plastic parts

### 1.3 Shakuhachi

#### PVC Shakuhachi (Japanese Bamboo Flute)
- **Material:** PVC pipe (ID: 20mm) + PVC joint pipe for mouthpiece
- **Source:** shaku6.com - detailed construction guide
- **Length:** 55cm total (54.3cm sounding length)
- **Hole positions (10mm diameter holes):**
  - Back hole: 7cm from top
  - Front holes: 24cm, 29cm, 34cm, 39cm from top
- **Mouthpiece:** Cut from PVC joint pipe at 45° angle
- **Key features:**
  - $1 cost, 30 minutes with drill
  - Sound quality comparable to genuine shakuhachi
  - Can use TUNER app for hole size adjustment
- **Variations:**
  - Monty Levenson: "Precision Cast Bore" using PVC as gauge for bamboo
  - Kurita Masami: Heated/pinched PVC with carved internal bore
  - Tapered bore possible by heating and shaping

### 1.4 Pan Flutes

#### PVC Pan Flute (Siku/Nai style)
- **Material:** Multiple PVC pipes of graduated lengths
- **Source:** Various (Make Studio JUMP, dp_makes on MakerWorld)
- **Design:** 
  - 8-15 pipes, closed at bottom end
  - Pipe diameters: typically 1/2" to 3/4" PVC
  - Glued or taped together in a row
  - Can be flat or curved arrangement
- **Tuning:** Length of each pipe determines pitch
  - L = v / (4f) for closed-open pipe
  - Example: Middle C (262 Hz) ≈ 33cm pipe length
- **Variations:**
  - Andean Siku: Two rows of pipes (interlocking)
  - Romanian Nai: Curved row of pipes
  - Chinese Paixiao: Vertical pipes in frame

### 1.5 Slide Whistles

#### PVC Slide Whistle
- **Material:** Nested PVC pipes (1/2" inside 3/4")
- **Design:** Piston slides inside outer tube
- **Source:** Stag 3D on MakerWorld (33.6K downloads)
- **Notes:** Also achievable with 3D printed design

### 1.6 Whistle/Tabor Pipe

#### PVC Tabor Pipe (3-hole)
- **Material:** 1/2" PVC pipe
- **Design:** 3 finger holes, fipple mouthpiece
- **Source:** pfh (Paul Harrison) Demakein
- **Notes:** Simple medieval pipe, played one-handed

## 3. Glissotar & Glissonic Family

### 3.1 Glissotar Overview
- **Inventor:** Dániel Váczi (saxophonist, composer, inventor)
- **Designer:** Tóbiás Terebessy (8+ years development)
- **Award:** First Prize + People's Choice, Guthman Musical Instrument Competition 2022
- **Website:** https://glissonic.com (sometimes down)
- **Glissonic concept:** Continuous pitch control via sliding ribbon/seal on bore
- **Applications:** Flute, recorder, clarinet, saxophone, tarogato, oboe, cornett

### 3.2 Glissotar Models

#### Glissotar Purpleheart (Original)
- **Material:** Purpleheart tropical hardwood
- **Type:** Acoustic woodwind
- **Bore:** Conical
- **Mechanism:** Sliding ribbon inside bore
- **Sound:** Warm, rich, organic
- **Price:** Premium (sold out frequently)

#### Glissotar Jam (3D-Printed)
- **Material:** Bio-composite (3D printed)
- **Type:** Acoustic woodwind
- **Form/Function:** Same as Purpleheart
- **Sound:** Slightly brighter, more upper harmonics
- **Durability:** Robust against moisture anomalies
- **Source:** https://glissonic.com/shop/
- **Note:** This is the accessible version

### 3.3 Glissonardo
- **Based on:** Leonardo da Vinci's Codex Atlanticus fissure flutes
- **Mechanism:** Continuous slit instead of discrete holes
- **Play:** Cover slot with closed hand, open from pinky to index
- **Range:** Full octave with smooth glissandi
- **Files on Printables:** STL + 3MF + STEP (free download)
- **Sound demo:** https://youtube.com/shorts/NSQ5zJAe3m4
- **Part of broader exploration by Dániel Váczi**

### 3.4 Glissopipe
- **Concept:** Recorder-style form factor with glissonic system
- **Target:** Beginners, musicians, educators, families
- **Status:** Launching on Kickstarter
- **URL:** https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/gryllussamu/glissopipe-intuitive-acoustic-instrument
- **Website:** http://www.glissopipe.com/

### 3.5 Glissonic YouTube Channel

## 4. Flute Extension Designs

### 4.1 Saxophone Low A Extension
- **Purpose:** Extends Bb baritone sax range to low A
- **Design:** 6" tube (4" inner diameter PVC), inserted into bell
- **Materials:**
  - 4" ID Schedule 20 or 40 PVC pipe (~$10)
  - ABS or PLA for 3D printed adapter
  - Foam weatherstrip seal
  - PVC cement (optional)
- **Assembly:** Press-fit with foam weatherstrip seal
- **Sources:** YouTube DIY saxophone modification tutorials

### 4.2 Bass Clarinet Low C Extension
- **Purpose:** Extends bass clarinet range from low Eb to low C
- **Design:** 3D-printed detachable U-tube sections
- **Each section:** Adds one semitone down
- **Bore:** ~30-35mm diameter, ~15-20cm per semitone
- **Source:** NYU student projects, various academic papers
- **Materials:** PLA/PETG body, neoprene pads, stainless steel rods

### 4.3 Bassoon Bocal / Crook
- **Purpose:** Transfers reed vibration to bassoon body
- **Design:** Conical bore metal extension
- **Heckel system numbering:** #1 (shortest) to #5 (longest)
- **Length:** ~30cm
- **Taper:** From ~4mm (reed end) to ~12mm (body end) internal diameter
- **Materials:** Brass or German silver (traditional), 3D printed prototype possible
- **Variations:**
  - Heckel system
  - CVS system
  - Moosmann bocals

### 4.4 Flute Low Bb Foot Joint
- **Purpose:** Extends concert flute range by one semitone to Bb3
- **Design:** Extension of C foot joint by ~2cm additional tube
- **Materials:** Nickel silver or sterling silver (traditional)
- **Notes:** Standard on most modern Boehm flutes

### 4.5 Clarinet Barrel Extension
- **Purpose:** Lowers pitch by semitones
- **Design:** Lengthened barrel section
- **Open register key design** for alternative fingering
- **Materials:** 3D printable, PVC, or traditional grenadilla

## 8. Design Notes for Instrument Designer

### New Instruments to Add
Based on research, these instruments should be added to the library:

1. **PVC Shakuhachi** — Simple construction, $1 cost, proven design
2. **PVC Native American Flute** — Fipple design, easy to play, 3D printed air director
3. **3D Printable Folk Flute Set** (pfh) — Soprano/Alto/Tenor, Demakein-generated
4. **Glissotar Jam** — 3D printed continuous-pitch instrument
5. **Glissonardo** — Da Vinci-inspired slit flute
6. **Glissopipe** — Recorder-form glissonic instrument
7. **PVC Pan Flute** — Multiple pipes, graduated lengths
8. **Saxophone Low A Extension** — PVC tube + adapter
9. **Bass Clarinet Low C Extension** — Modular U-tube sections
10. **Bassoon Bocal/Crook** — Conical bore extension

### Acoustic Considerations
- PVC Schedule 40 is food-safe (white), Schedule 200 is NOT suitable for corks
- PVC bore is smooth, reducing turbulence vs bamboo
- End correction: L_eff = L + 0.6r (open end), where r = pipe radius
- Frequency formula: f = v / (2L) for open-open, f = v / (4L) for closed-open
- Speed of sound: ~343 m/s at 20°C
- Tuning by ear with tuner app is most reliable method

### 3D Printing Tips
- 0.12mm layer height for best surface finish
- 100% infill mimics wood density (important for resonance)
- 4+ walls for rigidity
- Print flutes in segments for no supports needed
- Glue + waxed thread wrapping for airtight joints
- PETG or PLA both work; PLA is easier to print

### PVC Construction Tips
- Deburr all cut edges
- Sand interior of holes smooth
- Use food-grade white PVC only
- Drill slowly to avoid cracking
- Progressive drill bit sizes (don't skip sizes)
- Mark center line before drilling finger holes
- Use tuner app during construction for real-time feedback

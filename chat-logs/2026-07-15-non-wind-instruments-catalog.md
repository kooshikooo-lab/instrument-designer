# Non-Wind Instruments Catalog — Saved for Future Reference
**Date:** 2026-07-15
**Purpose:** Catalog all non-wind instrument research encountered during wind instrument development, for potential future expansion of the Instrument Designer app beyond wind instruments.

---

## CATEGORY 1: STRING INSTRUMENTS

### Circle Guitar
- **Creator:** Anthony Dickens (UK)
- **How it works:** Programmable wheel strums strings, player presses lightly to control
- **Bypasses:** manual strumming limitations
- **Creates:** new kinds of guitar music impossible with traditional technique
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — resonance chamber printable, strings and wheel need traditional materials

### Harpejji
- **Inventor:** Tim Meeks (2007)
- **How it works:** Strings laid flat on board, played by tapping (like keyboard layout but vertical)
- **Sound:** Blends piano versatility with guitar feel
- **Significance:** Intuitive for both pianists and guitarists
- **3D print potential:** Low — long strings under high tension, requires sturdy body

### Adjustable Microtonal Guitar
- **Creator:** Tolgahan Çoğulu (2014 Guthman winner)
- **How it works:** Custom fret spacing for any scale
- **Significance:** Proves fret-based instruments can be microtonal
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — fretboard and bridge printable, strings need traditional materials

### Mulatar
- **Creator:** Lockruf Music (UK/Germany)
- **How it works:** Slide guitar + harp + percussion hybrid, moving bridges
- **Placement:** 2nd Place + People's Choice, 2025 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Low — complex mechanical parts

### Sharpsichord
- **Creator:** Henry Dagg
- **How it works:** Solar-powered pin-barrel harp, plays programmed sequences
- **Appearance:** Victorian contraption
- **Sound:** Metallic, chiming tones
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — barrel and frame printable, strings need traditional materials

## CATEGORY 2: PERCUSSION INSTRUMENTS

### Mbira / Kalimba
- **Origin:** Zimbabwe, Africa
- **Scale:** Heptatonic, often "stretched" beyond Western octave. Most common: Nyamaropa (similar to Mixolydian)
- **Design:** 17-22 metal tines on wooden/plastic board, tuning screws
- **Tuning:** Each tine can be tuned independently — any scale possible
- **3D models:**
  - Thingiverse: "Tunable chromatic mbira/kalimba" by muradyan_75
  - MakerWorld: "Tunable 17-key Kalimba" (C3-E5 range)
- **3D print potential:** HIGH — board and tine holders fully printable. Tines can be steel or printed PLA (lower volume but works).

### Nyunga Nyunga
- Smaller mbira, 7-8 tines
- Subset of mbira family

### Babel Table
- **Creator:** Jean-François Laporte (Canada)
- **How it works:** Latex membranes + compressed air → voice-like sounds (percussive to birdsong)
- **Placement:** 2nd Place, 2024 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — membrane housing printable, membranes need latex

## CATEGORY 3: ELECTRONIC INSTRUMENTS

### Chromaplane
- **Creators:** Passepartout Duo & KOMA Elektronik (Italy/Germany)
- **How it works:** Electromagnetic field instrument, isomorphic layout, fully analog
- **Placement:** 1st Place, 2025 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Low — electromagnetic components need traditional manufacturing

### Thales
- **Creator:** Nicola Privato (Italy)
- **How it works:** Electromagnetic sensors for continuous pitch control
- **Placement:** Finalist, 2024 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Low — sensor technology

### X.E.K.I.
- **Creators:** Zhang, Chen, Kofinakos (USA)
- **How it works:** Motion-responsive keyboard for physical expression
- **Placement:** Finalist, 2024 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — keyboard housing printable, electronics need traditional

### YUAN
- **Creator:** Chi Wang (USA)
- **How it works:** Data-driven controller: capacitive touch + brightness + motion
- **Placement:** Finalist, 2024 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — housing printable

### Eigenharp
- **What it is:** Electronic instrument with 120+ keys
- **Plays:** hundreds of sounds, loops, effects in real-time
- **Significance:** Bridges electronic and acoustic music boundaries
- **3D print potential:** Low — complex electronic assembly

### Ondes Martenot
- **Inventor:** Maurice Martenot (1928)
- **How it works:** Electronic instrument with keyboard + ring on wire
- **Sound:** Eerie, wavering tones
- **Usage:** Film scores (Lord of the Rings, Ghostbusters), classical compositions
- **Significance:** One of the earliest electronic instruments still in active use
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — ring and keyboard housing printable, electronics need traditional

### Theremin
- **How it works:** Hand movements in air control pitch and volume (no touch)
- **Sound:** Eerie, wavering — synonymous with 1950s sci-fi
- **Significance:** First fully electronic instrument, played without physical contact
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — antenna and housing printable, circuitry needs traditional

### Sonògraf
- **How it works:** Audiovisual instrument: drawing/collage → sound via camera
- **Placement:** Finalist, 2024 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Low — camera-based, not mechanical

### ModμMIDI
- **Creators:** Emily Koh et al. (Singapore/Georgia)
- **How it works:** Microtonal MIDI controller
- **Placement:** Judges' Commendation, 2025 Guthman
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — housing and buttons printable, electronics need traditional

## CATEGORY 4: FLUID-STATE INSTRUMENTS

### Hydraulophone
- **Inventor:** Steve Mann (1985, patented 2011)
- **How it works:** Water jets → player blocks jets with fingers → pressure changes → sound
- **Sound:** Rich, fluid, gurgling. Calming and expressive.
- **Classification:** New category — liquid-state acoustic instrument
- **Variants:**
  - Reed-based (moving parts)
  - Reedless/fipple (no moving parts, simpler maintenance)
  - Electric (hydrophone amplification + MIDI)
  - Water-in-air, air-in-water, water-in-water
- **Range:** 12-88 jets, fully polyphonic
- **Applications:** Music therapy, public art, science centers, interactive installations
- **Notable installations:** Ontario Science Centre (world's largest), Legoland California, Copenhagen Experimentarium
- **Between Music:** commissioned custom hydraulophone for Aquasonic underwater concert series
- **3D print potential:** HIGH — jet plates, manifold systems, resonant chambers all printable. Requires pump system (not printable).

### Glass Armonica (Crystallophone)
- **Inventor:** Benjamin Franklin (1761)
- **How it works:** Spinning glass bowls played with dampened fingers
- **Sound:** Ethereal, ghostly — "once thought to drive performers mad"
- **Modern variant:** Between Music commissioned underwater version by Andy Cavatorta
- **3D print potential:** Low — glass bowls cannot be 3D printed. Housing and mechanism printable.

### Sea Organ
- **Inventor:** Nikola Bašić (2005, Zadar Croatia)
- **How it works:** Ocean waves → tubes under marble steps → polyphonic music
- **Classification:** Architecture as instrument — played by wind and sea
- **Significance:** Permanent public installation, no player needed
- **3D print potential:** Moderate — individual tube modules printable, full installation is architectural scale

### Waterphone
- **How it works:** Bowed metal rods in water chamber
- **Sound:** Horror film staple — eerie, metallic
- **3D print potential:** Low — metal rods and water chamber need traditional materials

## KEY INSIGHTS FOR FUTURE EXPANSION

1. **Mbira/Kalimba** is the most viable non-wind instrument for 3D printing — fully parametric, any tuning, board + tines both printable
2. **Hydraulophone** components are printable but require external pump — could be a "print the instrument, buy the pump" model
3. **Electronic instruments** generally need traditional electronics — but 3D-printable housings/cases are a valid accessory market
4. **String instruments** are limited by string tension — printable bodies but traditional strings
5. **Microtonal fretboard** (Adjustable Microtonal Guitar) principle applies directly to our wind instrument work — parametric fret/hole placement for any scale

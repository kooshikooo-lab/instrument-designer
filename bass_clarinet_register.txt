=== Bass Clarinet Register Physics ===
Model: cohere/north-mini-code:free

**1. Over‑blow interval**  
- **Result:** A **12th** (third harmonic).  
- **Physics:** For a cylindrical tube closed at the reed end the resonant frequencies are odd multiples of the fundamental ( f₁, 3f₁, 5f₁ …). The second register is the 3rd harmonic → interval = 19 semitones (octave + fifth).  
- **Example:** C₂ (≈ 61.7 Hz) over‑blows to G₄ (≈ 185 Hz).  
- **Source:** Rossing & Fletcher, *The Physics of Musical Instruments*, 4th ed., 2004, p. 212.

**2. Register / speaker‑hole placement**  
- **Rule of thumb:** Position the register (speaker) hole at **≈ 1⁄3 of the total tube length from the reed end**.  
- **Typical bass clarinet (L ≈ 1500 mm):** Hole ≈ 500 mm from the reed (≈ 1000 mm from the bell).  
- **Why:** This location coincides with the pressure node of the 3rd harmonic, giving a clean octave‑jump.  
- **Source:** Rice, *The Clarinet*, 1999, p. 78.

**3. Multiple register vents**  
- **Traditional design:** Single vent – the left‑hand thumb key (≈ 1⁄3 L).  
- **Modern designs:** Many professional bass clarinets (e.g., Selmer Custom II, Yamaha YBS‑1000, Meyer series) add a **second vent** near the bell (≈ 2⁄3 L, ~ 1000 mm from the reed, ~ 500 mm from the bell) to improve altissimo intonation and facilitate the “third‑register” (high‑F♯/G).  
- **Result:** Two‑vent systems give a more symmetric pressure node for the 5th harmonic, reducing over‑blow difficulty.  
- **Source:** Meyer, J., *Modern Bass Clarinet Design*, 2005, p. 42.

**4. Cut‑off frequency for a 23.5 mm diameter cylindrical bore**  
- **First higher‑order (radial) mode cutoff:**  
  \[
  f_c = \frac{c}{2\pi}\frac{2.405}{a}
  \]
  with \(c≈343 \text{m s}^{-1}\), \(a = d/2 = 11.75 \text{mm}=0.01175 \text{m}\).  
- **Calculated:** \(f_c ≈ 11.2 \text{kHz}\).  
- **Practical range:** **≈ 11 – 12 kHz** (the first non‑planar mode).  
- **Implication:** All planar (axial) modes below ~11 kHz are well‑supported; higher‑order modes begin to appear above this frequency.  
- **Source:** Kinsler et al., *Fundamentals of Acoustics*, 4th ed., 2000, p. 215.

**Take‑away numbers**

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Over‑blow interval | 12th (19 semitones) |
| Register hole position | ≈ 1⁄3 L from reed (≈ 500 mm on a 1500 mm bass clarinet) |
| Additional vent (modern) | ≈ 2⁄3 L from reed (≈ 1000 mm) |
| Cut‑off frequency (23.5 mm bore) | ≈ 11.2 kHz (≈ 11–12 kHz) |

These figures give a quick reference for design work or acoustic modeling of bass clarinets.
